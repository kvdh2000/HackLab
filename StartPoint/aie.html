Adventures in Eximagdra

The world of Eximagdra came to be from a cultivation of many different things and thoughts I had over the years prior.

In 2019 I learned that some of my new college friends were also into D&D. I myself had at this point never actually played the game before, but was very familiar with it. When I was a child, my parents would often play it with their friends, and I often watched, and read through the source books. Now, having learned my friends were into it, we set out to start an adventure. We went around and looked into who would be the Dungeon Master (DM), and we landed on me. And thus, I started working and thinking.

The story I came up with was based on a short story I had written a few months prior. The story was about a man who found himself suddenly in a dark cave, chasing and being hunted by a giant Demon-like Wolf. He was an adventurer on a quest, just like a D&D player character. I at this point started writing out this short story into an introduction for our campaign, weaving together some loose character ideas I had, setting up lore behind what the Demon-Wolf was, and what had to be done about it. This setting turned into a valley between some mountains, where the initial campaign was set.

As we started playing, I worked ahead to a bigger picture. This valley, now named Western Valley, got expanded to have a lot more lore and different types of peoples. Then I started expanding beyond. The Plains of Ruthal to the East, a savage land of Goliath and Dragons. Mountain's Reach to the North, where the Dwarves were from. Crescent Bay, a bustling region of primarily Humans. Moonsong Woods in the far North, where the Moon Elves dwelled. And so it grew and grew, region by region, race by race, deity by deity.

Over a course of two years it was fleshed out into a full continent, each region with it's own cities, culture, religion, and everything. The initial campaign took them through much of the South-West of the continent, and after about a year of play, a second campaign was started, taking them further North. And then in 2019, when I went to a differnet study, and made new friends, I started 4 more campaigns, some set in the same regions, some set on the other side of the continent. And with every step the world grew richer in lore and story, turning into a true open world fantasy that I could run as many campaigns in as I wanted.
